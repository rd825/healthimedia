Founder & Executive Producer

Paula M. Levine

An award-winning writer and producer with more than 20 years of experience in broadcast, cable and on-line, Paula has produced and developed news and feature pieces, documentaries, talk shows, and web stories of all sorts. But her real passion is for all things Health and Lifestyle.

She has reported on every subject from the Ironman Triathlon to Alzheimer’s to Kids with Disabilities to Food and Wine. Along the way, she has worked with pharmaceutical companies, networks, and non-profits. No matter what the subject or length, her gift for storytelling can bring it to life. Paula has run 7 New York City marathons, including two with the Achilles Track Club for Disabled Runners.

Director of Photography

Joseph Friedman

Joe is a Manhattan-based Director of Photography with over 3 decades of experience shooting documentaries, corporate stories, interviews, and narrative features. His clients have included HBO, PBS, NBC, CBS, and ABC. He has shot in 26 countries and worked with scientists, dirt farmers, Wall Streeter’s, transsexual prostitutes and presidents; and covered topics as diverse as brain surgery, grinding poverty, and babies coming into the world. Joe brings a special sensitivity and intimacy to everything he shoots. That special talent won him the Edward R. Murrow Award for Journalism.

Editor

Ken Sackheim

Ken is an editor, filmmaker, composer and a co-owner of Work Edit, a Post-Production facility in mid-town Manhattan. His work includes everything from feature films and commercials, to documentaries and animated projections that sync with orchestral performances. He has worked with Paula on literally hundreds of feature stories, interview segments, and on-going series. Their story on Living with Alzheimer’s won an Emmy Award.

Writer & Graphic Designer

Claire Lingham

Claire is a writer and graphic designer who loves to take an inspired dream and turn that dream into a reality in unique and creative ways. Since getting her start in New Zealand, she has taken her talents across the country and around the world. She is passionate about the pursuit of a healthy and balanced lifestyle; and combining this passion with her work. She believes there’s more to being healthy and happy than just the size of our physical bodies. It’s about finding that happy balance with physical, social, mental/emotional, and spiritual health. It is what the Maori, native New Zealanders, call “Hauora”.

Nutrition Consultant and Founder, HealthBarn USA

Stacey Antine

Stacey is a registered dietitian who holds a Master’s of Science in Food, Nutrition and Dietetics from New York University. Alarmed by the long list of lifestyle diseases faced by children today, she founded HealthBarnUSA to connect children to natural foods and where they come from. After 7 years of empowering more than 30,000 kids to eat and live healthy, she is now sharing her proven approach on PBS and Nickelodeon; and with her book, Appetite for Life: The thumbs-up, no-yucks guide to getting your kid to be a great eater.

Mental Health Expert

Emily Grossman

Emily is a Recovery Specialist and Trainer for the Center for Practice Innovations at Columbia Psychiatry. She works with people suffering from mental illness, and helps them to build happy, fulfilling lives. In addition, she has built a life coaching practice in NJ called, The Dream Team Coaches. She first worked with Paula two years ago on a series of personal profiles for Everyday Health about people living with Bi-Polar Depression. Emily holds a Masters in Education, a CPRP (Certified Psychiatric Rehabilitation Practitioner), and is currently working on her Doctorate in Psychiatric Rehabilitation.
